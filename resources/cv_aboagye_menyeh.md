## Professional Summary

Professor Aboagye Menyeh is a distinguished geophysicist with over four decades of experience in research, teaching, and academic administration at KNUST. His pioneering work on the magnetic properties of monoclinic pyrrhotite (Fe₇S₈) has established him as an international authority in mineral magnetism, with 517+ citations and an h-index of 12. Beyond his research contributions, he served as the Foundation Provost of the College of Science (2004-2010), transforming the institution through strategic leadership, international partnerships, and infrastructure development. The College of Science building was named the "Aboagye Menyeh Complex" in 2015 in recognition of his transformative contributions.

## Research Impact

Professor Menyeh's work on monoclinic pyrrhotite has had lasting impact on the field of mineral magnetism. His experimental studies on domain structures, coercive force mechanisms, and magnetization processes established new theoretical frameworks that continue to be cited decades after publication. His applied research on gold exploration and groundwater prospecting has contributed to Ghana's natural resource sector, with practical applications in mineral exploration and water resource management.

His administrative leadership transformed KNUST's College of Science into a world-class institution, with enhanced research infrastructure, international partnerships, and academic programs that continue to benefit students and faculty. The naming of the Aboagye Menyeh Complex stands as a permanent testament to his institutional legacy.
